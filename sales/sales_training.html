1. Executive Summary & The Opportunity

Indian restaurants — from single-kitchen dhabas to 20-branch chains — are squeezed between aggregator commissions (typically 18–30%), thin margins on dine-in, and no ownership of the customer relationship. Every order placed on Swiggy or Zomato leaves the restaurant with a receipt; it does not leave them with a customer they can re-message.

GullyBite is a WhatsApp-first commerce operating system that lets restaurants take direct orders, unify every aggregator and POS into one dashboard, and keep the customer relationship they are already paying for.

Why WhatsApp, why now

  • WhatsApp Business is already the most trusted channel with Indian diners — no new app to download, no friction.

2. The Restaurant Owner's Pain Points

Before you walk into a meeting, know what keeps the owner awake. These five pains are the hooks your pitch attaches to.

2.1 Aggregator commissions eat the margin

A ₹500 order on an aggregator can net the restaurant ₹320–₹380 after commission, payment gateway, and promotion. On a 12% net margin business, that is the entire week's profit on one order.

2.2 They do not own the customer

A restaurant that has served 8,000 orders on Swiggy does not have 8,000 phone numbers. When the aggregator raises commission or throttles visibility, the restaurant has no way to reach its own diners.

2.3 Operations are fragmented

Swiggy tablet. Zomato tablet. Dunzo handset. POS screen. Phone for walk-in reservations. Each has its own login, tone, and dispatch flow. Orders get missed. Staff get confused during rush.

2.4 Repeat-order marketing is manual or non-existent

"Send a WhatsApp to last week's customers" means one staff member, 2 hours, zero personalisation, and no tracking. Campaigns that do happen cannot be attributed to revenue.

2.5 Delivery logistics are a headache

In-house riders are expensive and idle between peaks. Ad-hoc delivery is unreliable. Porter, Dunzo, and local 3PLs each have separate flows and no tracking visibility for the customer.

Listen for these phrases in discovery. "Swiggy is killing us." "I wish we had their numbers." "My staff are running between tablets." "I tried WhatsApp marketing once, never tracked anything." Any one of these is a qualified opening.

3. The GullyBite Solution

GullyBite is a four-layer platform. You should be able to draw this on a napkin.

3.1 Customer layer — WhatsApp-native ordering

  • Catalog inside WhatsApp. Customer browses menu via a catalog message or interactive list — no app install.
  • Meta Flows for heavy lifts. Address capture, location sharing, cart review, and reorder all run as native WhatsApp Flows, not clunky web redirects.
  • Razorpay checkout. UPI, cards, wallets. Order is "paid" before the restaurant sees it — which eliminates fake orders and COD disputes.
  • Loyalty and referrals baked in. 4 tiers (bronze → silver → gold → platinum) and a shareable GBREF referral code every customer gets automatically.

3.2 Restaurant layer — unified dashboard

  • One inbox for every order — direct WhatsApp, Swiggy, Zomato, POS-originated — with the same accept/decline/dispatch flow.
  • Persistent order notifications. A paid order triggers a looping sound + modal on the dashboard until staff explicitly accept or decline. No missed orders during rush.
  • Accept / decline with automatic refund. Declining a paid order triggers a Razorpay refund automatically — the owner never has to remember to do it manually.
  • Branch-level control. Menus, pricing, availability, and staff permissions scoped per outlet for multi-branch chains.

3.3 Integration layer — live today

POS

Petpooja · UrbanPiper · DotPe — catalog & order sync. Additive: keep your POS, get a customer channel.

Aggregators

Swiggy · Zomato — orders flow into the same GullyBite inbox. We do not replace them; we unify them.

Delivery (3PL)

Porter integration with live tracking links sent to the customer over WhatsApp.

Payments

Razorpay orders, webhooks, automatic refunds on decline, settlement engine for payouts.

3.4 Growth layer — what drives repeat

  • Abandoned-cart recovery. Customer drops off mid-order → automated nudge on WhatsApp.
  • Post-order rating request with gating: 4–5 stars get a Google review deep link, lower scores route back to the manager privately.
  • Coupon templates + referral attribution so every marketing rupee is traceable to a customer and an order.

5. Illustrative Case Studies

ILLUSTRATIVE SCENARIOS — the following are representative composites to help you tell the story. Do not quote specific numbers as guaranteed outcomes.

Case A — The single-outlet QSR "Biryani House"

  • Before: 180 orders/day, 70% via aggregators at ~23% blended commission. Monthly aggregator commission ≈ ₹2.4L.
  • After 90 days: 40 orders/day migrated to direct WhatsApp (own customer list built from rating flow + coupon templates). Aggregator volume held; direct orders were incremental from repeat diners.
  • Illustrative P&L impact: ~₹65–80k/month margin recovered on those 40 direct orders + ~3,200 phone numbers owned and remarketable.

Case B — The 6-branch chain "South Spice"

  • Before: 6 branches, 6 Swiggy tablets, 6 Zomato tablets, one central manager juggling screenshots over WhatsApp groups. No central view of performance.
  • After 60 days: All 6 branches on a single GullyBite dashboard with branch-scoped permissions. POS sync via UrbanPiper live in 2 branches. Central manager views one inbox.
  • Illustrative operational impact: missed-order rate (internally tracked) dropped significantly; direct-WhatsApp revenue reached mid-single-digit percent of total in the first quarter.

Case C — The dark kitchen "Late Night Noodles"

  • Before: 100% aggregator-dependent. One bad visibility week = 40% revenue drop.
  • After 45 days: Cart-recovery + loyalty drove repeat direct orders from existing aggregator customers who had opted into WhatsApp. Porter integration replaced ad-hoc delivery for the direct channel.
  • Illustrative resilience impact: when aggregator visibility dropped the following month, direct-WhatsApp orders buffered ~25% of the shortfall. Owner described it as "an insurance policy that also makes money."
How to use these in the field. Pick the one closest to the prospect's size and tell it as a story: problem → what they tried → what changed → where they are now. Do not quote percentages as promises; always say "owners like you have seen outcomes in this range."

6. Sales Positioning & Pitch Framework

6.1 The 30-second intro

"GullyBite is a WhatsApp-first ordering and operations platform for restaurants. You keep Swiggy and Zomato. On top of that you get (a) a direct WhatsApp ordering channel with zero commission, (b) one dashboard that unifies aggregators, POS, and direct orders, and (c) a customer list you own and can remarket to. Most outlets go live in under a week."

6.2 Discovery questions — ask these in order

  1. How many outlets do you run and how many orders per day per outlet?
  2. What's your rough aggregator commission as a % of GMV right now?
  3. How do you currently track repeat customers or run promotions?
  4. What POS do you use, and is it something your staff actually use fully?
  5. What's the one thing about your current setup that frustrates you most during the dinner rush?

6.3 Demo flow — 12 minutes

  1. Customer view (3 min): demo an actual WhatsApp order end-to-end on a demo number. Browse, add to cart, pay, receive confirmation.
  2. Restaurant view (4 min): show the paid order arriving with sound + modal, accept it, show the dispatch to Porter with live tracking link, then show the customer side receiving that link.
  3. Unified inbox (2 min): show a Swiggy-originated and a direct-WhatsApp order side by side in the same dashboard.
  4. Growth view (2 min): loyalty tiers, referrals, cart-recovery flow, rating request.
  5. Multi-branch / permissions (1 min): only if prospect is a chain.

6.4 Closing framework — the "two lists" close

"Today you have one list — Swiggy's. In 90 days with GullyBite you'll have two lists — theirs and yours. Which one of those do you want to depend on next year?"

  1. Ask current order volume and average order value.

8. FAQ & Objection Handling

Q: "I already have Swiggy and Zomato. Why do I need this?"
A: You keep them. GullyBite is additive. It gives you a commission-free direct channel and pulls aggregator orders into one screen. The customer list you build on WhatsApp is yours — and that's the asset aggregators never give you.
Q: "My customers won't order on WhatsApp."
A: That's exactly what owners said three years ago about paying on UPI at a restaurant. WhatsApp is already the #1 customer-service channel in Indian F&B. We're just adding a payment and catalog layer to a conversation you're already having.
Q: "What if a customer pays and I can't fulfil the order?"
A: The dashboard has one-click decline that automatically refunds via Razorpay. No manual refund process, no customer dispute calls. The system won't even mark the order cancelled unless the refund succeeds first.
Q: "Will this work with my POS?"
A: Petpooja, UrbanPiper, and DotPe are live today. If you're on one of those, catalog and orders sync automatically. If not, you can run GullyBite standalone and export daily.
Q: "How do I get customers onto WhatsApp in the first place?"
A: Three channels: the rating flow captures numbers from every delivered order (including aggregator); your referral codes are shareable; coupon templates incentivise the first direct order. Most outlets have their first 100 WhatsApp customers in week one.
Q: "What about delivery? I don't have riders."
A: Porter is integrated. Orders auto-dispatch, the customer gets a live tracking link on WhatsApp, and the delivery cost is transparent. No fleet to manage.
Q: "Is this another long contract lock-in?"
A: Pricing is monthly. There's a 30-day success checkpoint. No hidden per-order fees on the direct channel.
Q: "Who owns the customer data?"
A: You do. Every phone number, order, and preference sits in your GullyBite workspace. Exportable any time. That's the whole point.

10. Your 30-Day Action Plan

This is how a new sales rep goes from onboarded to first closed deal.

Week 1 — Absorb

  • Complete the demo-restaurant walkthrough on your own WhatsApp. Place three orders. Accept, decline (trigger refund), dispatch.
  • Shadow two discovery calls and two demos with a senior rep.
  • Memorise the 30-second intro (Section 6.1) word-perfect.

Week 2 — Prospect

  • Build a list of 40 target outlets within 5 km. Mix: 20 single-outlet, 15 two-to-five-branch, 5 larger chains.
  • Cold-walk 10 outlets/day in the 3–5 PM lull window. Goal: 10 discovery conversations by end of week.
  • Book at least 4 demo appointments.

Week 3 — Demo and Diagnose

  • Run 4 demos. Record what objection came up in each. Bring the two toughest to weekly sales review.
  • For every demo, do the ROI calculation on paper with the owner (Section 7).
  • Send a written follow-up within 24 hours — the ROI page and the case study nearest their size.

Week 4 — Close

  • Push at least 2 demo prospects to commercial conversation with the success manager.
  • First target: 1 Starter or Growth plan signed by day 30.
  • Stretch target: 1 chain in active evaluation.

Daily rituals that separate top reps

  1. Morning 15-minute review of the previous day's demo notes. What objection blocked progress? Practise the counter aloud.
  2. Keep a "live deal" WhatsApp group with your manager — post every meaningful signal (re-opened proposal, question about integration, referral intro).
  3. End every demo by asking "what would need to be true for you to sign this week?" Listen. That sentence is the close.
One closing thought. You are not selling a SaaS login. You are selling a restaurant owner the chance to own their customer list — probably for the first time in their business life. Treat every pitch with the weight that deserves.
